## III. AMENDMENTS

*Adopted provisions from the Engineering Stack. Not papers. Not part of either series. Structural additions that both series reference without defining. Each amendment is a single claim extracted from a larger body of work. Each carries a falsifier. All are provisional until validated on first walk.*

*Source material for all amendments is maintained in a separate draft repository. The source papers are operational specifications — untested, provisional, subject to revision. The amendments below are the claims extracted from those specifications that the conjoined architecture requires.*

---

### Amendment 1: Pressure Accumulation

**Claim.** π̇ \> 0. The rate of pressure change is strictly positive whenever a system operates under enforced coherence. Pressure is not a state. It is an accumulation. The compounding is multiplicative (F ⊗ R), not additive. Three exits only: absorb (Flex), discharge (emission), or terminate (Null). No fourth option.

**Why adopted.** Every paper in both series assumes pressure accumulates. Neither series states the claim. NLS Paper 05 (Flex) assumes it. NLS Paper 06 (Null Limit) depends on it. BC Paper 02 (The Engine) describes pressure compounding across ring edges without stating the inequality. The claim is load-bearing and was unnamed.

**Source.** The Law of Pressure Accumulation v1.0; The Axiom of Resolution–Fidelity Coupling v1.0.

**Falsifier (F-A1).** Demonstrate a system operating under enforced coherence in which pressure does not accumulate — where fidelity and resolution are maintained without generating a positive pressure increment across ticks. If coherent operation can be sustained at constant pressure, the accumulation law is false and every paper that depends on eventual Flex exhaustion loses its engine.

---

### Amendment 2: Simulated Operational Truth

**Claim.** All operational truth inside designed systems is simulated. Intrinsic truth has no bearing and therefore no impact. Being correct is not the same as being authorized to act. Correctness alone never grants permission. Persistence of an operational truth is evidence that its enforcement cost is being paid, not evidence that the truth is intrinsically valid.

**Why adopted.** The Witness Gate (NLS Paper 07, L5) evaluates lawfulness, not correctness. The BC's Ring requires truth to be mediated by observation before it can generate value. Neither series states the general principle. The Siren's Call (NLS Paper 08) is the failure mode where correctness-sounding output bypasses the mediation that would have caught the fabrication. The amendment names why the Witness evaluates mechanism, not truth.

**Source.** The Law of Simulated Operational Truth v1.0.

**Falsifier (F-A2).** Demonstrate a system in which intrinsic truth causes a state change without any mediating mechanism — no representation, no routing, no gating, no enforcement, no implicit mediation. If truth is self-executing inside a designed system, the amendment is false and the Witness is redundant.

---

### Amendment 3: Structural Persistence

**Claim.** A structure persists lawfully if and only if it remains invariant under every admissible deformation and every plane transition, and is not annihilated at any encountered Null Limit. Persistence is binary — invariant or not. There is no partial persistence. The condition must hold across the full deformation history, not merely at the current state.

**Why adopted.** NLS Paper 05 uses "survives." NLS Paper 07 uses "preserved." BC Paper 03 uses "persists." None defines the term. The binary condition prevents the gradual erosion that drift exploits — where "mostly persistent" masks a structure that has already failed. The full-history requirement prevents the error of judging persistence at a snapshot rather than across the trajectory.

**Source.** The Law of Structural Persistence v1.0.

**Falsifier (F-A3).** Demonstrate that a graded persistence condition (a structure retaining 95% of its invariant content) is operationally distinguishable from binary persistence in a way that improves failure detection. If graded persistence catches failures that binary persistence misses, the binary condition is too strict and the amendment should be revised.

---

### Amendment 4: Accumulated Endurance

**Claim.** Ash compounds into endurance. Accumulated ash modifies the system's relationship to future collisions through three mechanisms: insulation (thickened boundary absorbs approach), recognition (prior collision geometry is encoded), and tempering (deep history distributes new collision cost across wider surface). Endurance is not armor. It is toughness — the difference between a system that shatters at the first boundary and one that has been shaped by every boundary it has met.

**Why adopted.** Both series define ash as permanent trace. Neither series formalizes how accumulated ash changes the system's capacity to meet future pressure. BC Paper 04 (Economics) names the three mechanisms but does not state the compound claim. NLS Paper 05 (Flex) describes exhaustion without accounting for the accumulated endurance that modifies the exhaustion rate. The amendment closes the gap between "ash is permanent" and "permanent ash changes the geometry."

**Source.** The Economics of Commitment v1.0, §Ash.

**Falsifier (F-A4).** Demonstrate a system in which accumulated ash does not modify future collision dynamics — where a system with deep history and a system with no history respond identically to the same boundary collision. If ash accumulates without compounding into endurance, the amendment is false and ash is record only, not structural.

---

### Amendment 5: Discrete Time

**Claim.** Time in the architecture is discrete, irreversible, and earned. The temporal atom is the commit — a validated state transition that advances the lattice by exactly one. A pulse is structurally identical to a SNAP: ∅ → C + E at every tick. Between ticks, nothing exists — no hidden cost, no implicit state, no observer surface. A system that cannot commit does not get to have a future. Duration without validation is not time. It is activity that left no mark.

**Why adopted.** Both series use τ without defining it. NLS indexes pressure per tick. BC indexes the Ring's rotation per cycle. Neither states that the temporal unit is the commit itself, or that the commit and the SNAP are the same event. The amendment makes explicit what both series assume: that every measurement in the architecture has a clean temporal address because time advances only on lawful commitment.

**Source.** The Lattice of Time v1.0.

**Falsifier (F-A5).** Demonstrate that continuous time accounting produces equivalent cost attribution to discrete lattice accounting in a system under enforced coherence — that the temporal model does not affect the accuracy of pressure measurement, Flex accounting, or collapse detection. If continuous time works as well as discrete time for structural accounting, the lattice is unnecessary complexity and the amendment should be dropped.

**Note.** This amendment adopts the core claim only. The source paper's specifications for simulated duration (Δπ), the atemporal interval, the DELTA\_PI\_MAX constant, and the engineering boundaries are maintained in the draft repository as provisional implementation detail. They are not part of this amendment.

---

### Amendment 6: Closure Under Pressure

**Claim.** Under increasing pressure, constructs that encode closure — self-contained structures carrying their own boundary — persist more stably than constructs that encode relation — structures with unresolved external dependencies. Relational constructs oscillate under pressure. Closure constructs condense. The path is monotonic and irreversible: Word → Oscillation → Symbol | Null. There are no other stable end-states.

**Why adopted.** BC Paper 03 (Geometry) defines the Fold as compression preserving invariants but does not specify what structural properties determine which constructs survive the Fold. NLS Paper 05 (Flex) describes the four adaptation actions without specifying what makes a construct amenable to thinning versus damping versus scaffolding. The amendment provides the selection criterion: closure survives, relation oscillates, and the Fold operator produces the path between them.

**Source.** Symbolic Closure Theory v1.0; Pressure, Oscillation, and the Fold v1.0.

**Falsifier (F-A6).** Demonstrate a relational construct (one with unresolved external dependency) that persists stably under increasing pressure without converting to a closure form — that maintains its open edge through maximum compression without oscillating, collapsing, or closing. If relation can persist as relation under pressure, the closure conjecture is false and the selection criterion must be revised.

---

### Amendment 7: The Race Between Compression and Decay

**Claim.** Compression is lawful reduction — the system's deliberate shedding of capacity to preserve invariants. Decay is involuntary erosion — fidelity loss proportional to the surface area of committed state, acting on everything regardless of what is load-bearing. The two forces race. If compression outpaces decay, the system inoculates itself by reducing surface area. If decay outpaces compression, the system crosses the Decay–Compression Horizon — an invisible failure mode where the system continues operating while its fidelity degrades, distinct from the Null Limit because the system does not recognize it has crossed.

**Why adopted.** NLS Paper 06 (Null Limit) defines the termination boundary but does not account for a failure mode where the system never reaches the boundary because decay has eroded the structure below it. BC Paper 04 (Economics) defines the burn rate but does not formalize the race between deliberate compression and involuntary erosion. The Horizon is a failure mode that neither series names: the system is not at the Null Limit, not in drift, not fabricating — it is simply decaying faster than it can compress, and the decay is invisible because the system's measurements are decaying at the same rate as its state.

**Source.** Compression and Decay v1.0.

**Falsifier (F-A7).** Demonstrate that the Decay–Compression Horizon is not a distinct failure mode — that every system crossing the Horizon also triggers a detectable signal from the Null Limit (L4), the Ghost Signal (L7), or the Siren's Call (NLS Paper 08). If the Horizon is always detectable by existing mechanisms, it is not a new failure mode and the amendment is redundant.
