## Purpose

This document catalogs every falsifiable claim in the Bias Cascade.
Each entry includes the claim, the formal statement, the paper and
section where it appears, and the exact observation that would break it.
Any single falsifier is sufficient to collapse its target. If the target
is foundational, the collapse propagates upward.

The index is organized by dependency. Level 1 falsifiers, if triggered,
collapse everything. Level 5 falsifiers, if triggered, break only the
self-validation layer. Work from the bottom up if you want to destroy
the architecture. Work from the top down if you want to find the weakest
link.

Every claim is listed as Open. This is not a claim that the falsifiers
have been tested and survived. It is a claim that they have been stated
and are available for testing. The architecture stands until a falsifier
holds. The invitation is permanent.

## How to Report a Falsifier

If you believe you have triggered a falsifier:

1. **Cite the falsifier ID** (e.g., F-G5).
2. **State the counterexample:** what you observed, constructed, or demonstrated.
3. **Explain why the architecture cannot absorb it** — why it is not a special case, a misinterpretation, or an edge condition within Flex.
4. **Identify the cascade:** which levels collapse if this falsifier holds.

A falsifier that holds is not an attack on the work. It is the work functioning as designed. The architecture was built to be broken honestly. A tear that cannot be repaired means the architecture was wrong at that point. A tear that reveals a hidden assumption means the architecture was incomplete. Both are better than an architecture that cannot be tested.
